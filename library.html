The Library

Conversations

From January to July 2020, as a follow up to the labs and the symposium, the members of the network recorded a series of conversations around themes that emerged during our activities. These were informal, sometimes with some questions prepared in advance, sometimes just with a couple of words to rekindle what was going on when we were together in 2019. Mostly just three or four people sharing questions, concerns, aspirations and sometimes anger. A lot of them happened during mid 2020’s lockdown, a provocative context for thinking about performances of intimacy, and the cause of huge disruption to the companies and artists involved, so that inevitably becomes a point of interest and speculation too.

1. Intimacy

Martina Seitl | Christer Lundahl | Bruce Barton

Martina, Christer and Bruce talk about the creation of intimacy for audience participants. Beginning with two questions that had figured in both lab sessions: how to prepare participants, and the degree of control artists aim for in interactions, the conversation encompasses the use of technology and ritual, techniques of guiding, a dramaturg’s role in designing participation and how people with different sensibilities and neurodiversities engage.
